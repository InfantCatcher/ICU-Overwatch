The Clinical Problem

The ICU Deterioration Challenge

Current diagnosis is reactive rather than preventive. Sepsis and organ failure are typically identified only after clinical symptoms have fully manifested.

In intensive care units, sepsis and sudden organ failure develop rapidly. Delayed identification compounds resource strain, prolongs hospital stays, increases patient mortality, and places immense pressure on clinical staff and public healthcare finances.

Our Solution

Standardised Predictive Panel

We built an early warning system monitoring a panel of 42 physiological variables drawn from routine, non-invasive ICU telemetry.

By continuously evaluating real-time clinical markers, our algorithm outputs predictive risk alerts across five distinct endpoints. This lead time gives medical teams a critical window to intervene before active organ failure clinically manifests.

Clinical Integrity

Model Performance & Validation

To ensure real-world generalisability, the model was trained on 210,013 patients and evaluated on an independent external cohort of 29,579 patients across hospitals in geographically distinct settings.

ROC Curve Preview
AUROC: 0.932

Internal Cohort (210,013 patients): Robust training baseline across MIMIC-III, MIMIC-IV, and eICU. Sepsis AUROC reached 0.845; Liver Failure reached 0.982.

Pediatric Cohort Transparency: Performance was more modest in the pediatric cohort (AUROC 0.798). Dedicated calibration is required before pediatric clinical application is considered.

Hospital Utility

Why This Matters for Hospitals

Easing clinician burden in high-stress intensive care environments.

Hospital environments face acute systemic issues: staffing shortages, nurse burnout, high patient-to-practitioner ratios, and unexpected emergency bed overflows (as seen during COVID-19 surges).

Our continuous predictive warning system helps alleviate these pressures by replacing simple, noisy threshold alarms with a single, highly specific dashboard that triggers only when intervention is clinically necessary.

Increase Nurse Capacity

Eliminates simple telemetry alarm fatigue. Staff are directed only to patients showing true, sub-clinical deterioration signs, streamlining shift tasks.

Prevents Bed Backlog

Reducing clinical ICU length of stay by 10% frees up critical beds. This eases emergency department wait lists and optimizes patient throughput.

Mitigates Bed Overflow Crisis

During surges or pandemics, continuous monitoring provides an objective safety net, allowing nurses to safely monitor more patients without losing care quality.
